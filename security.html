How to Tackle Fraud?

Here are some ways in which you can tackle Fraud:

  • Keeping your personal and financial information protected and secure through responsible information collection and processing.
  • Protecting against threats with an award-winning cybersecurity team that delivers comprehensive security round-the-clock.
  • 24/7, real-time monitoring for suspicious account activity.
  • Alerting you to potential fraud through the mobile app, text alerts, email or phone.

NOTE: Do not provide access codes, personal and financial information via email, text or to unsolicited callers. We will never call you to ask you for this information. If you receive an email or text from us and you're unsure if it's real, don't click on any links.


Know the warning Signs of Identity Theft

  • You notice unauthorized activity on your bank account or new accounts on your credit report.
  • You receive unexpected letters and calls from debt collectors about loans or other debt that you don’t owe.
  • You’re notified by the IRS that there’s a possible issue with your tax return, or that your return was previously filed in your name.
  • You’re alerted that your account was accessed from a new device you don’t recognize.
  • You’re billed for medical services you didn’t receive.
  • You no longer receive bills and bank statements in the mail.
  • You’re unexpectedly denied credit.

Red Flags of scam

  • Instructed to not trust Bank of America, or to respond to questions in untruthful ways
  • Pressured to send money
  • Threatened with law enforcement action
  • Told to purchase gift cards and provide codes as a form of payment
  • Asked to cash a check for a stranger or send money via transfer
  • Asked to deposit a check that overpays for something you’re selling, then send the difference elsewhere

What are the best ways to avoid being scammed?

  • Don’t respond
  • Don’t trust caller ID or answer phone calls from unknown numbers
  • Don’t give out your information
  • Research and validate

How can I protect my Account?

Here are some ways in which I can protect my account:

  • Keep your contact information up to date
  • Create the strongest possible passwords
  • Allow push alerts on the Mobile Banking app
  • Protect your devices
  • Control who has access to your accounts
  • Establish a cyber awareness program
  • Create a cyber security program and have an incident response plan
  • Enable biometrics (fingerprint sign-on or facial recognition)
  • Know the red flags that signal a scam
  • Know which third parties have access to your account information

Card and Transaction Security


What should I do when my Debit/Credit Card is missing or stolen?

  • Sign in to your online banking account to report your lost or stolen card. We'll cancel your card and send you a replacement immediately.
  • Notify any merchants who automatically charge your card and provide them with your new card information.
  • Review your transaction history for suspicious activity, and contact us immediately if there are transactions that you did not make.

What should I do when my checkbook is lost?

  • Contact Us or provide the number of the last check that was written or the name of the person or business to whom it was written.
  • Sign in to Online Banking to view digital copies of your checks and to see if any unauthorized checks have been written to your account.
  • Schedule an appointment to visit a financial center so you can close your current account and open up a new one (if you have any recurring charges set up to your checking account, be sure to update those charges with your new account number once you receive it).
  • If checks you wrote haven’t been cashed at the time your checkbook was lost or stolen, you may need to contact the payees and send them checks from your new account.

What should I do when I see a suspicious transaction on my bank statement?

  • Contact the merchant directly.
  • You can file a dispute for credit card charges by signing in to Online Banking

What should I do if I become a victim of ID Theft?

  • Contact your financial institutions and creditors.
  • Check your credit reports and place a fraud alert on your file.
  • File an identity theft report and retain it for your records.
  • Watch out for suspicious emails, phone calls or text messages asking you for your personal information.
  • Review "How to Tackle Fraud".

What should I do when I recieve a suspicious email, text message or phone call?

  • Forward any suspicious email or text message to us.
  • We will never ask you in an email, text message or phone call to give us personal information such as your account number or card PIN.
Look out for Phishing emails which has the following contents:
  • Often the message doesn’t address you by name. It also implies urgency, attempting to get you to act quickly before you have time to carefully read the message or examine it thoroughly.
  • If you hover over a link in a phishing email, it will usually show you that it’s pointing to a site different from the one stated in the message. The goal is to get you to click through to a web page where you’ll be asked to provide personal information or open an attachment that may be malicious.
  • Phishing messages often contain grammar and/or spelling errors.
